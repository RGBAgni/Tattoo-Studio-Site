FAQ

how do i book an appointment?
slide into our contact form, give us the goods (what you want, size, placement), and we’ll get back to you with times and a quote.
do you take walk-ins?
yes! you can come without an appointment on friday's, between 10 am and 5pm. it's advised to come early to avoid disappointment!
how much will my tattoo cost?
depends. tiny and simple? probably less. big and detailed? a bit more. you can see what each artist charges per hour on their site pages, however it's best to come in for a chat and we’ll give you a price range once we see your idea.
what about piercings?
piercing prices depend on the difficulty of the piercing, material required, etc. gice us a call and we'll be happy yo walk you though it!
do i need to pay a deposit?
yup. it locks in your spot and shows you're serious. it also covers the time we spend prepping your design. it’s non-refundable.

can i bring my own design?
absolutely. bring your weirdest sketch or your most sacred pinterest board. just know we might tweak it so it actually works on skin.
can i get tattooed if i’m pregnant or breastfeeding?
nope. we’re not taking chances with your health or your tiny human. wait it out.
how should i prep before my session?
eat something, stay hydrated, wear comfy clothes that let us get to the spot you’re getting inked. also, please shower.
what if i’m running late?
text us. we’ll try to keep your spot if you’re under 15 minutes late. after that, we might reschedule and keep the deposit.
can i bring a friend?
sure, bring one hype person. but no squads, no filming crews, no group chats in human form.

do tattoos hurt?
yes. some spots more than others. but if you can survive high school gym class, you can handle this.
how do i take care of my new tattoo?
gently wash it, pat it dry, and keep it moisturized with a boring, fragrance-free lotion. no picking, no soaking, no tight clothes rubbing it.
can i go swimming after?
nope. stay dry for 2-3 weeks. pools, lakes, hot tubs — all off-limits unless you’re into infections.
is peeling normal?
yep. it's like a bad sunburn but prettier. don't pick at it. your tattoo will look crusty before it looks cool.
when can i work out again?
give it a couple days. if it’s in a spot that moves or sweats a lot, give it longer. your fresh tattoo isn’t into gym life just yet.
can i go in the sun?
not unless you wanna fade that fresh ink. cover it up or stay in the shade. sunscreen comes later, once it's fully healed.
what if my tattoo looks weird while healing?
healing stages are ugly. dry, patchy, dull-looking — totally normal. if it still looks weird after a few weeks, hit us up.
can i use ointments like neosporin?
please don’t. that stuff can trap bacteria and mess with your ink. stick to what we recommend — fragrance-free lotion or specialized aftercare.
what if i think it’s infected?
if it’s super red, swollen, hot, or leaking anything funky — reach out. if it’s sketchy, see a doctor. no shame in staying safe.

can i park near the studio?
short answer: no. long answer: seriously, don’t try. there’s no parking near the shop. plan to walk a bit, or uber if you're fancy.
what if i change my mind?
cool, but your deposit's still gone. our artists already put in time. be sure before you book — this isn’t a lunch order.
how do i care for my new piercing?
clean it twice a day with saline solution. no twisting, no touching with dirty hands, and definitely no changing the jewelry too soon.
my piercing looks angry — is that normal?
a little redness and swelling is fine. crusties are normal. but if it’s throbbing, oozing yellow stuff, or smells like regret — hit us up.
